Discover the World of Microgreens

Microgreens are tiny plants packed with powerful nutrients. Learn how they grow, why they matter, and how to enjoy them in your daily meals.

What Are Microgreens?

Microgreens are young vegetable or herb seedlings harvested soon after germination—when the seed leaves (cotyledons) and usually the first true leaves appear. Most varieties are ready in 7–21 days. They’re tender, intensely flavored, and naturally colorful.

Why They’re Nutritionally Special

Microgreens concentrate key micronutrients and bioactives per gram (variety-dependent) — including vitamins C/E/K₁, carotenoids (β-carotene, lutein, zeaxanthin), chlorophylls, and (in crucifers) glucosinolates like glucoraphanin.

  • Heart health: potassium, magnesium, and vitamin K help support healthy blood pressure and arterial function; red-cabbage microgreens show cholesterol-lowering and anti-inflammatory potential in animal models.
  • Cancer risk (supportive): cruciferous microgreens (broccoli, radish, cabbage) provide glucosinolates → sulforaphane, studied for upregulating detox enzymes and protecting cellular DNA.
  • Eye health: lutein + zeaxanthin (e.g., spinach, amaranth, pea shoots) support the macula and help filter high-energy blue light.
  • Immune support: vitamin C (e.g., cilantro, red cabbage) and vitamin E act as antioxidants; diverse polyphenols help limit oxidative stress.
  • Blood sugar: early human studies with broccoli microgreens suggest potential to improve fasting glucose; fiber and bioactives may aid glycemic control.

Evidence spans lab/animal work to small human trials. Microgreens complement a balanced diet and medical care — they’re not a cure.

Sprouts vs. Microgreens

  • Sprouts: grown in water, usually in the dark; harvested in 2–5 days; you eat seed + root + shoot.
  • Microgreens: grown on a medium (coco/soil/mats) with light; harvested in 7–21 days; you cut the shoot above the medium.
  • Different growing methods → different textures and nutrient profiles. Both are great; microgreens excel in color, flavor, and plate appeal.

How We Grow

1. Preparation

Trays are filled with a clean, consistent medium (e.g., coco). Tools and surfaces are sanitized.

2. Seeding

Seeds are evenly spread for uniform density and airflow across the canopy.

3. Blackout

Short, dark “press” phase encourages strong rooting and even germination.

4. Light & Air

Trays are uncovered, provided with ample light and gentle airflow for sturdy, green growth.

5. Harvest

Cut just above the medium with sanitized tools; pack cold for maximum freshness.

Safety & Storage

Our Hygiene Basics

  • Trusted seed lots; clean water
  • Sanitized trays, tools, and surfaces
  • Good airflow + adequate light
  • Cold chain from harvest to handover

At Home

  • Keep chilled, dry, and unwashed in clamshells
  • Wash just before eating
  • Enjoy within 3–7 days for peak crunch, color, and aroma

How to Use Microgreens

  • Finish hot dishes after cooking (retain texture & aroma)
  • Top salads, bowls, wraps, omelettes
  • Smoothies: a small handful boosts color & micronutrients
  • Try flavor profiles: sunflower (nutty), radish (peppery), pea shoots (sweet), broccoli (mild)
